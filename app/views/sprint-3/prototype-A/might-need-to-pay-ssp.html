You might need to pay Statutory Sick Pay

Your employee has been off work for 42 calendar days.

You must pay Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) if your employee is eligible. SSP is £96.35 a week, and you pay it instead of a normal salary. You cannot claim SSP back from the government.

Use our online form to calculate how much SSP you need to pay.

Before you pay SSP you can ask your employee for proof of their sickness. A fit note is an example of proof. Fit notes used to be called sick notes.

Your employee can ask their doctor for a free fit note if they've been off sick for 8 or more days. Find out what to do when you’re given a fit note.

Paying SSP is a legal requirement if your employee is eligible. Some employers also choose to pay additional sick pay. If you offer extra sick pay you can decide when to start and stop paying it, and how much extra to pay.
